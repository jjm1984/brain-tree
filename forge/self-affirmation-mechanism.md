  ```
  The Identity Paradox: Why 'I Can't' is Less Powerful Than 'I Don't Want To'. "I can't eat that" (external restriction, temporary, forced, implies you want to but something stops you) vs "I don't eat that" (internal identity, permanent, chosen, ends discussion). Study cited: 8x more likely to stick to goals, less temptation, no internal conflict — "Your brain believes what you tell it." Examples: "I can't eat sugar" vs "I don't eat sugar"; "I can't skip my workout" vs "I don't skip workouts"; "I can't eat after 8pm" vs "I don't eat after 8pm." "'I can't' = fighting yourself. 'I don't' = being yourself."
  ```

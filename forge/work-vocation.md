  ```
  REVENUE | Total sales for the period. | $500,000 | Look for trends in revenue growth over consecutive periods to gauge business performance.
  COST OF GOODS SOLD | Beginning inventory + purchases - ending inventory. | $310,000 | Comparing COGS with revenue helps in understanding gross margin.
  GROSS PROFIT | Revenue - Cost of goods sold. | $190,000 | It reflects the efficiency of your production process and is a precursor to measuring profitability.

  OPERATING INCOME | Gross Profit - Operating Expenses. | $40,000 | Operating income gives you a snapshot of your company's operational efficiency before other costs and taxes.
  INTEREST EXPENSE | Interest paid on loans or debt. | $10,000 | Account for non-operating items like interest income, interest expense, and other gains or losses that aren't related to the primary business activities.
  NET INCOME BEFORE TAX | Operating Income - Interest Expense | $30,000 | Add or subtract the other incomes/expenses to/from the operating profit to calculate pre-tax income. this is the company's earnings before tax.
  INCOME TAX EXPENSE | Tax Rate (20%) * Net Income Before Tax | $6,000 | Deduct taxes to find the net income. this is the actual profit after all expenses, including taxes, have been subtracted from total revenue.
  ```

  ```
  HOW TO LEAD TEAMS THROUGH CHANGE
  
  1. Forming — High dependence on leadership, excitement mixed with caution, unclear roles, and low performance as the team starts out. Tips: provide clear direction and set expectations for roles and tasks; encourage open communication; establish a framework for collaboration early to build trust and reduce uncertainty. Teams may revisit this stage during transitions.
  
  2. Storming — Conflicts emerge as roles are established, with challenges to leadership and potential "turf wars." Tips: coach the team to handle conflicts and resolve power struggles constructively; promote open dialogue and listen actively; establish clear conflict resolution processes. Conflict, though uncomfortable, is essential for growth.
  
  3. Norming — Conflicts are resolved, trust builds, and collaboration improves as roles become clearer. Tips: facilitate teamwork and shared decision-making; delegate responsibilities more freely; celebrate small wins and recognize achievements. Teams stabilize here, but may take longer if Storming conflicts aren't properly resolved.
  
  4. Performing — Peak performance with autonomous, competent team members who require minimal supervision. Tips: delegate authority and trust the team's independent decisions; focus on long-term objectives and encourage innovation; monitor performance but maintain a hands-off approach unless needed. The team may briefly regress under new challenges.
  
  5. Adjourning — The team disbands after achieving its goals, often with mixed emotions of accomplishment and loss. Tips: celebrate achievements; conduct post-project reviews to gather lessons learned; support team members through the transition. Recognition and reflection provide closure.
  ```

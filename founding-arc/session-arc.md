# The Founding Session Arc

The tree began with a question about the body. It ended with a question about the mind being built.

**Opening question:** How can I hack my body to be the best version of me?

**The reframe:** The question behind the question — how do I align my physical life with who I'm trying to become, given real constraints? 'Hacking' implies working around a system. But if the body is a representation of the inner self, the real question is alignment, not optimisation.

## Journey markers

1. Body hacking → philosophy of embodiment
2. Fitness → triad built for daughter, then recognised as needed for self
3. Triad → philosophy and action coexist
4. Consistency gap → grit courage problem
5. What feeds grit → self-worth question
6. People are for connection → most of all to be understood
7. Building the tree → the 'dear reader' moment: writing for an audience is an act of wanting to be understood
8. Crossroads principle named in dialogue
9. Four philosophical tensions worked through
10. Ethics → grudge/resentment distinction introduced by J
11. Forgiveness arc completed
12. Courage deserves its own section
13. Session ended: what kind of mind are you building? Seven knowledge domain candidates named.

**Closing line:** You started asking how to hack your body. You ended asking what kind of mind you're building. That's a good session.

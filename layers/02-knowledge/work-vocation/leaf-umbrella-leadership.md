# Umbrella Leadership

J's own coined term. Tested in the military, applied at SwarmFarm. Sits at the intersection of caring-worth and coherence-worth: looking after your people so they look after the customer, so the business/outcome follows — trust chain, not separable ledgers.

**Principle:** the leader absorbs organisational threat, politics, and blame so the team's energy goes toward deliverables, not self-protection. Focus stays on the work; the leader cops the difficulty upstream of the team.

## Named downsides (J's own)

**Invisibility** — a multiplier who isn't seen until they're not there. The glue that holds a team together, then people leave once the protection stops. Structural, not incidental.

**Politics becomes a skill, not a concept** — protecting people from organisational politics requires the leader to become genuinely skilled at politics themselves, not stay above it. This creates a real fork: either you get taken out, or you end up playing the game you were trying to shield your team from. Held open as a crossroads, not resolved — tension between directness/ethics and strategic necessity.

## Convergent traditions, not source

**Simon Sinek — *Leaders Eat Last*, "Circle of Safety"** — near-identical structure, independently arrived at by J via lived military/corporate experience, not adopted from the book. Leaders absorb external threat so team energy goes inward to the work rather than self-protection; sourced from actual Marine Corps mess-hall practice (leaders eat last). Real evidence base, not just anecdote: the Whitehall Studies found workplace stress correlated with lack of CONTROL, not rank/responsibility — the actual mechanism behind why a protected team outperforms an exposed one. J confirmed he likes Sinek's take on this.

**Liz Wiseman — *Multipliers* vs. Diminishers** — adjacent but NOT identical. Wiseman's five disciplines (Talent Magnet, Liberator, Challenger, Debate Maker, Investor) are about intellectual amplification, making the room smarter. J's pattern is closer to political/organisational protection plus tactical translation — a distinct mechanism, not a subtype of Wiseman's five. Gap in Wiseman's framework, flagged as J's own contribution rather than borrowed: neither Wiseman nor Sinek names the invisibility cost J identified — Wiseman's focus is upward (what the leader extracts from others), Sinek's is inward (team cohesion); neither centers what the LEADER forfeits by doing this well.

## Counter-pattern: credit-hoarding (observed in others, not autobiographical)

Credit-hoarding / visibility-protection via work-hoarding, including worst-case credit-taking for collaborative work — a real pattern J has observed repeatedly in high-performing teams among mid-tier professionals, **not autobiographical for J.** An earlier draft of this session incorrectly proposed discarding this observation when J indicated it wasn't a personal pattern; J corrected this — observed patterns in others are legitimate tree content distinct from autobiographical patterns, and should not be dropped just because they aren't self-referential.

This is the classic worth-machine version of visibility-management in leadership — the failure mode umbrella leadership and Wiseman's Multiplier archetype both stand against. Logged here as the standing counter-pattern to this leaf, not as a separate node.

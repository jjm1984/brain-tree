Generalizes leaf-skill-audience-motivation-mapping from a single listener to N stakeholders in the same room/conversation.

**Task:** in a room with multiple stakeholders who each want something different, work out what each one is actually motivated by, and pitch the same project/idea to all of them concurrently — not sequentially, not as a compromise, but by finding how one initiative genuinely serves each party's distinct goal.

**Decision procedure:** (1) identify every stakeholder's underlying motivation, not their stated position; (2) hold all of them in view at once rather than optimising for one at the expense of the others; (3) find and articulate the specific way the current project advances each party's own goal; (4) use that multi-front alignment to gain buy-in and resources — the alignment IS the mechanism that unlocks resourcing, not a side effect of it.

**Tacit judgment:** J explicitly separated two things usually collapsed into one 'people skill': (a) the *capability* — being able to read what motivates a given role — comes from having actually occupied that role, at some level, at some point; (b) the *will* to do it — actually deploying that capability instead of just pursuing your own agenda — comes from what J calls a 'circumspect frame of mind': a way of attending that doesn't get stuck in your own motivations, and actively reaches for what could be motivating the others at the table. Capability without that disposition would just be accurate manipulation; the disposition is what keeps it aligned toward genuine mutual advancement.

**Voice sample (J's own words):** "The skill to do that comes through having experienced being in their role at some point and at some level. But the desire to do it is actually through a circumspect frame of mind that doesn't get stuck in your own motivations. It accesses what could be motivating the others at the table."

**Concrete evidence base:** not abstract — J's own career index (branch-work-vocation) shows him having occupied nearly every position in the ag-tech value chain at different times: hands-on mechanic/end-user, dealer-level GPS specialist, BI vendor, startup founder, enterprise vendor (Trimble, APAC scale), robotics field-ops leadership, and now moving into OEM-level marketing (AGCO). The translator skill's capability side is directly explained by this role-diversity, not a separate talent running alongside it.

**Cross-connection to leaf-inner-witness:** genuinely strong, non-speculative. 'Circumspect frame of mind that doesn't get stuck in your own motivations' is functionally the same move as the Inner Witness's mindfulness dimension — observing without identification — applied socially rather than internally. Where the Inner Witness watches the self's autopilot without being fused to it, the translator disposition watches the self's own agenda without being fused to it, freeing attention to actually perceive others' motivations. This may be the same underlying capacity (the witness) showing up in two directions — inward at the mycelium/habit layer, outward at the social/stakeholder layer.

**Open question (sharper version of the one already open on philo-people-before-process):** J described the circumspect frame as specifically NOT getting stuck in his own motivations — attention moves outward, toward others. Worth asking directly: does that same circumspect attention ever turn reflexively onto J's own motivations, examined with the same rigor applied to everyone else at the table? Or does the technique work partly *because* it routes attention away from self-examination in the moment? Not answered here — named as the sharper version of the question already open.

Named specifically in sales/customer-facing contexts — Trimble, SwarmFarm, dealer/farmer interactions.

**Task:** in a live conversation, working out why the other person is actually there and what they're hoping to get, then adjusting language and approach in real time to speak to that.

**Decision procedure:** put yourself in the listener's frame first — farmer, customer, dealer. Identify their underlying motivation before pitching anything. Reframe the conversation around solving THEIR problem, not around the product's own features or virtues. The product is a tool in the hands of the person who buys it, not something with value in itself.

**Tacit judgment:** named directly as a rejection of a common failure mode in the same field: too many salespeople sell a product for its own virtue and benefit. J names this as both less effective and not how he operates — the product only matters insofar as it helps the other person do their work better than they're already doing it.

**Voice sample (J's own words):** "A product is only a tool, and it's a tool in the hands of the person that buys it to complete the work in a better, more valuable way than they're already doing."

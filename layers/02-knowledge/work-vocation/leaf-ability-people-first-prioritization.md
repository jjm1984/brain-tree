Cross-role, not tied to a single job — named explicitly across civilian work, leadership, and military team performance.

**Task:** deciding what to act on first when competing demands appear — an admin task vs. a customer issue, a policy vs. a client in need.

**Decision procedure:** customer-facing/people-facing need always outranks administrative task. Client-in-need always outranks policy/process/guardrail. As a leadership rule: look after the people, they look after the customers; look after the customers, they look after the revenue. The causal chain runs people → customers → revenue, not the reverse — revenue and process are treated as downstream effects, never as the thing being optimised directly.

**Tacit judgment:** named explicitly by J as a North Star spanning career, not just a single job — visible in civilian leadership style, in how J operates within high-performing military teams, and in one-on-one customer/farmer/dealer interactions.

**Voice sample (J's own words):** "It's people first. Processes and rules and guidelines, guardrails, none of that matters. It's people that matter. Look after the people first, and they look after the customers. Look after the customers, and they'll look after the revenue."

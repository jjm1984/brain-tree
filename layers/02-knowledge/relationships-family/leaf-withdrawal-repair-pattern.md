# Withdrawal as Flight/Freeze, and the Seven-Step Repair Pattern

Withdrawal is reframed as a single flight/freeze mechanism on a spectrum, not two separate mechanisms — where "intentional, protecting my position" vs. "unintentional, nervous-system overload" sit is determined by how much self-awareness is available in the moment, not by a fixed rut-track.

## Cause-no-harm resolution

Total avoidance is not the only Trunk-compliant option. A clearly stated need that causes some pain can still satisfy "cause no harm" if it passes the four conditions of conscious sacrifice (named / proportionate / capacity-preserving / articulable — see sub-conscious-sacrifice), applied here to speech rather than sacrifice.

## The seven-step repair pattern (tested, lived)

1. Be aware the pattern exists.
2. Notice the pattern inside yourself and others.
3. Recognise the pattern inside conflict — the hardest, most load-bearing step, most likely to fail silently.
4. Regulate — tell the nervous system what is happening.
5. Gain emotional regulation, or reach out for support/space to do so. Two sub-mechanisms, not one: regulate-alone vs. signal-that-regulation-is-underway — the second doubles as an early repair-trigger signal to the partner so "taking space" doesn't read as further withdrawal.
6. Align together against the problem, not against each other (Gottman framing).
7. Solve and/or repair.

**Household-specific context:** undiagnosed neurodivergence (others in the household) plus hyper-independence means "wait for a better state" cannot be assumed to happen automatically via normal social cues — a shared, external, pre-agreed repair trigger is needed precisely because in-moment self-report can't be relied on from either side.

**The faster-regulating partner's role**, named precisely and left as the closing line, not reduced further: they regulate themselves, then extend regulation to the other while often still hurting themselves, unresolved. This is functionally identical in outward behaviour to a parent-child dynamic (managing rather than meeting as an equal) — the difference is not visible from outside, only in whether competence is offered in service of returning to equal footing or in place of it. In J's own words, not reduced further: "it truly takes love."

# Marriage as the Site of Intentional Change — Patriarchal/Matriarchal Balance

The first real content planted in branch-relationships-family, promoting it from fully peripheral to yellow.

J's observation: in his culture, the patriarchal line tends to set family direction more than the matriarchal line — not because it's inherently more important, but as a cultural pattern. J's own words: inherited traits are "just as important through patriarchal and maternal lines" — the asymmetry is cultural, not evidential. J states an explicit intention to shift this pattern in his own generation, having married "a strong and accomplished woman who doesn't hide behind me."

Held as J's own observation and stated value-commitment, not adjudicated as a general sociological claim. The observation (cultural asymmetry) and the intention (to change it in his marriage) are two separate things worth keeping distinct: the first is a claim about the world, the second is a personal commitment regardless of whether the first is universally true.

# The Personal Triad — Happy, Strong, Healthy

**Happy** — Engaged, feeling alive and fulfilled. Not surface contentment — aliveness. Deep in the nervous system, not a temporary state.

**Strong** — Stronger every day. Directional, not a fixed destination. Proven and noticeably so — not aspirational. Physical and mental.

**Healthy** — Right fuel in the body. A mind capable of change — able to deal with sorrow, grief, and stress without falling apart. Full of goodness and light. Not skinny, not blemish-free.

Originally built while helping daughter reframe body image. Then recognised as the framework needed for self. Notably absent: performance metrics, appearance standards, comparison to others. Virtue ethics in practice without having named it that.

**Inference:** The triad is the embodiment philosophy made actionable — and virtue ethics in practice before virtue ethics was named.

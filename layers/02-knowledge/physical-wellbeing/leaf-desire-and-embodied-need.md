# Desire as an Embodied Need

If the body is not a vehicle for the self but an expression of it (philo-embodiment), then desire - attraction, erotic aliveness, physical reciprocity - is part of what the body is expressing, not an appetite separate from identity to be managed or moralised. Wanting is not a flaw in the container; it is the container speaking.

This reframes desire's felt presence or absence the same way philo-embodiment reframes physical decline generally: not necessarily a referendum on virtue or worth, but data - the body reporting a state, the way the check-engine light reports rather than accuses. Desire present or absent is information about alignment, not a verdict on character.

## Where this sits in the tree

- **branch-aliveness**: one of the six conditions of aliveness is 'the body is part of it, not separate.' Desire and erotic aliveness are a direct instance of that condition, structurally the same as any other embodied form of aliveness (bushwalking, music).
- **leaf-triad**: 'Happy' is defined as 'deep in the nervous system, not a temporary state' - already open enough to hold this without amendment.
- **mycelium-hormones**: testosterone and oxytocin are already named as the biological substrate - drive/capability and bonding respectively. Desire operates at this level before it operates at the level of philosophy.

## Counters

**Buddhist non-attachment** (already a counter to philo-embodiment) - desire as the root of suffering, not a need to be honoured but a pattern to be released.

**Stoicism** - desire is a 'preferred indifferent' at most; equanimity does not depend on it being met.

## Exposed roots

- **Merleau-Ponty - phenomenology of the body** (already named under philo-embodiment) - extends directly: desire is part of the body's mode of being-in-the-world, not a separate drive layered on top of it. `yellow resonant, not yet engaged directly`

# Internal Inception — Imagination as Both Access and Counterfeit

Through movies and imagination the brain reverts to a younger, purer self, before the weight of the world — for a moment glimpsing what you wanted to be and what you could be. But the same spark of imagination leaves a mark like a pheromone telling the brain "you did it, you had the adventure your heart craves" — it was in the mind, but the heart knows it wasn't real, and it waits.

**Dual framing:** a way to access unsurfaced desires — the same surfacing mechanism as the boredom/stars image (mycelium-nervous-system) — imagination reveals what the heart craves before it's consciously articulated. But also a named danger (see counter): the imagined adventure can counterfeit the real one closely enough to drain the urgency for actual pursuit.

# Case: Woodworking — Test Case for Purposeful Purposelessness

J described woodworking (rustic practical furniture, self-assessed as 'not great,' valued highly by others) as enjoyable, process-led ('I let it tell me what it's going to become'), but consistently framed in output/production language ('producing something,' 'it's work'). J also named a broader pattern: choosing work itself as the enjoyable outlet, rather than seeking enjoyment outside of production.

## Two readings, unresolved

**Reading A — genuine doing/feeling fusion:** matches the trunk's own aliveness thesis that when something is truly alive, doing and feeling cannot be told apart. 'I only enjoy things that produce something' would then not be a deficit but a description of how aliveness shows up for J specifically.

**Reading B — worth-machine colonization of leisure:** doing-worth has expanded to cover all available time, leaving no activity exempt from needing to be useful/valued, which would mean purposeful-purposelessness is a genuine unexplored gap rather than something already being lived without being named.

## Diagnostic test (not yet answered)

Two questions: (1) During the activity, does time disappear (aliveness condition 1)? (2) Would the wanting to do it survive certain knowledge that no one — including J himself, later — would ever see or value the result? Yes to both points toward Reading A. A 'no' to either, especially the second, points toward Reading B and names purposeful-purposelessness as a real gap, not yet occupied.

See mycelium-default-mode-network and the updated leaf-reserves-restoration: nature/beach walking already confirms J has genuine access to purposeless reserve forms, which suggests woodworking may simply be a different category of good — not the same gap as this case originally implied, possibly no gap at all.

# Borrowed Peace

**When internal peace cannot be found, it can be borrowed from outside.** This branch started as a single leaf about landscape and indifferent order — the mountain that doesn't need you to be at peace, that simply is. That mechanism still holds, fully, as `leaf-borrowed-peace-indifferent-order`. But a second mechanism surfaced that borrows peace from a source that is *not* indifferent: a regulated person, extending regulation to one who isn't. The two are mechanistically opposite in one important way — indifferent-order peace costs the giver nothing (the mountain doesn't notice you); co-regulation costs the giver something real, every time.

## The two mechanisms

- **Indifferent-order peace** (`leaf-borrowed-peace-indifferent-order`) — peace borrowed from something that asks nothing back: landscape, an instrument, quiet. Free to the giver, because there is no giver in any relational sense.
- **Co-regulation** (`leaf-borrowed-peace-co-regulation`) — peace borrowed from a person. Not free. The regulated partner extends steadiness while, per `leaf-withdrawal-repair-pattern`, "often still hurting themselves, unresolved." A real cost, paid by a real person.

## Why this is one branch, not two unrelated leaves

Both are answers to the same underlying condition — internal peace not being available this season, per `leaf-peace-as-ingredient`'s own crossroads — routed through different sources. The mechanism differs; the need they answer does not. Keeping them siblings under one branch keeps that shared origin visible, while the state and cost-structure differences stay leaf-level, not smoothed away at the branch.

## Open note

Which mechanism a given moment actually needs is not always obvious in the moment — see the crossroads on `leaf-borrowed-peace-co-regulation` for how that fork is currently held open, deliberately unresolved.

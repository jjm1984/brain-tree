# Raising Kids Who Can Spot What's Healthy and Have Room to Become Who They Are

Not a skill for one moment. A skill for the hundreds of small moments,
across years, that add up to what a kid notices, trusts, and becomes.

## When this comes up

- A kid is describing a friendship or relationship, and something in
  how they describe it is worth gently naming.
- A kid is figuring out who they are, and the day-to-day has no room
  in it for that figuring-out.
- A kid needs an alternative to numbing out — boredom, a bad week,
  peer pressure toward the easy escape.
- You're deciding what to actually spend weekend time on.

This skill doesn't have a single trigger the way skill #1 does. It's
lived out over repetition, not resolved in one conversation — see the
reference for why that changes the shape of this skill.

## Two threads, not one sequence

Skill #1 has a "sequence, in order" because it's responding to one
moment. This skill has two separate teaching threads that don't share
a timeline — they run in parallel across a childhood, not one after
another. Treat them as two pillars, not five ordered steps.

### Pillar 1 — Recognising what's healthy

Teach the kids to recognise love bombing, gaslighting, and control —
not as clinical terms, but as patterns they can feel and name. Teach
them what a securely attached, well-adjusted person actually looks
like day to day, so the healthy pattern is as recognisable as the
unhealthy one — not just a list of red flags to watch for.

**Don't teach them to stay if they're not happy.** Staying is not the
virtue. But see the reference for the counter: this isn't permission
to bail at the first sign of friction either — every real relationship
has some.

Prioritise kindness, intelligence, and potential over the things that
are easier to see at a glance.

### Pillar 2 — Room to become who they are

Raise kids with room to actually explore who they are — not just who
they're expected to be. Connection to land and place. Working with
your hands, and the diligence that comes with it. A real place for
independence: bonfires, camping with mates, weekends on horses or
bikes — as the alternative that's already there when the easy escape
is going out to get drunk, not a lecture delivered after the fact.

## What this skill is not

Not a script to recite at kids. Not a checklist to complete once and
consider done. Not a guarantee — kids will still make their own
choices with what they're given. It's the raw material you hand them
early, so it's already there when they need it.

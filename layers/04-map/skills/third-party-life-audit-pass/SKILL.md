# Running a Life-Domain Audit in Third-Party Voice

A skill for the moment of doing a periodic life check-in — the tactic, not the strategy. For why this exists as a Map-level practice at all, see `map-life-domain-audit` (`layers/04-map/index.md`).

## When to use this

- Doing a periodic life check-in and it's starting to feel like a rubber-stamp — "yeah, fine, fine, fine" across every domain without real scrutiny.
- Suspecting that first-person self-assessment is being softened, avoided, or rationalized in a way you wouldn't extend to someone else's situation.
- Need a starting structure for an open-ended "how am I actually doing" question, rather than facing a blank page.

## The procedure

**1. Pick the domains.** Use this project's existing branches, not a new list: health/physical wellbeing, finances/wealth-freedom, work/vocation, relationships/family, self-worth/identity, aliveness.

**2. Write each domain in third person.** Not "I am currently..." — "He is currently..." / "She is currently...". Brief the assessment as if reporting on this person's life to someone else who needs the honest version, not the polished one.

**3. Score or flag each domain honestly from that external vantage.** The third-person framing is the entire mechanism — it's what lets the honest version surface. Don't let it collapse back into first person mid-pass.

**4. Switch back to first person only to decide.** Once a domain is flagged, drop the observer voice and decide one concrete action per flagged domain, in your own voice.

## What this skill is not

Not a fixed 8-area checklist — the specific domain list was discarded from the source material as generic and unattributed; only the third-person mechanism was kept. Not a substitute for care in flagged domains, only the diagnostic step that gets you to see them clearly enough to act.

# The OODA Loop — Breaking Execution-Stage Bogging-Down

TASK: Break analysis-paralysis or bogging-down at the tactical/execution level by cycling fast through Observe-Orient-Decide-Act instead of trying to fully resolve uncertainty before acting.

## When to use this

- Execution has stalled because a decision is being held for more certainty than the situation will provide.
- The same ground is being re-analyzed without new information changing the picture.
- A tactical, time-pressured decision needs to move, not be perfected.

## The procedure

**1. OBSERVE** — gather the current, specific state of the situation, not the state you assumed yesterday.

**2. ORIENT** — this is the step most models skip, and it's the one Boyd considered most important: filter the observation through your existing mental models, culture, prior experience, and genetic heritage — and explicitly ask whether THIS observation breaks one of those models rather than fitting neatly into it.

**Primary anchor for Observe + Orient (2026-07-19): `leaf-inner-witness`, not just the mycelium material below.** `leaf-inner-witness` is explicitly defined as spanning mycelium/OS/philosophy — "the capacity of the whole system to perceive itself" — which is Observe+Orient in different words. That leaf already uses "the soldier's battle rage — directed, choreographed, inhabited... a someone inside it" as an image for witness-during-intensity. Boyd built OODA from real aerial combat — not a loose metaphor match; both describe the same territory (high-stakes, threat-adjacent, witness-required states) from two registers: phenomenological (aliveness/inner-witness) and tactical (OODA).

**Caution on this step:** `mycelium-brain` and `mycelium-os-relationship` establish that amygdala hijack physiologically reduces pre-frontal cortex access — this is exactly why `audit-framework`'s tripwires must be pre-committed in clear-headed time, since "the hardware goes partially offline under the conditions that trigger it." OODA's core value is looping FAST, but fast-looping under amygdala hijack risks the worth-machine exploiting the very speed that makes the loop useful — Orient becomes unreliable exactly when Decide/Act feel most urgent. Do not treat OODA speed as unconditionally good; the same clear-headed-time precondition `audit-framework` already requires should gate this skill's use in high-stakes/high-urgency moments, not just its low-stakes tactical applications.

**3. DECIDE** — commit to the smallest reversible next action, not the largest correct-sounding plan.

**4. ACT** — execute, then immediately re-enter Observe on the result. The loop's speed matters more than any single iteration's correctness.

**Note:** this is the mechanism that specifically answers "execution is where people get bogged down" — bogging down at Execution is usually a symptom of trying to fully resolve Decide before acting, instead of trusting the next loop iteration to correct course.

**Documented precedent (2026-07-19):** `leaf-urgent-vs-important` (physical-wellbeing) already documents this skill's core problem in one domain: "Physical training has repeatedly been displaced by urgent demands. Recognising this as a structural pattern, not a willpower failure." This is a concrete, already-lived precedent for Execution-stage bogging-down — a candidate for generalizing beyond the physical-training domain specifically, rather than treating this skill's fix as untested.

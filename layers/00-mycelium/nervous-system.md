# Nervous System — The Operating Layer

The autonomic nervous system is the body's operating layer beneath conscious choice. It runs the body without asking permission. Understanding its two modes — and the specific configuration your system has trained itself into — is foundational to understanding why the philosophy works when it works and fails when it fails.

## Two modes

**Sympathetic** — fight, flight, freeze. Cortisol and adrenaline. Urgency, action, heightened alertness. For this nervous system: cortisol is not an alarm — it is the ignition. The sympathetic state is the operating baseline. Eustress dependency.

**Parasympathetic** — rest, digest, restore. The recovery state. For a cortisol-dependent system, this state does not arrive voluntarily — it is enforced by removal of demand (leave, transition, illness). The crash is the parasympathetic reset the system won't take voluntarily.

## Polyvagal layer

Stephen Porges' framework. The vagus nerve as the biological mechanism of safety, connection, and restoration. Three states: ventral vagal (safe, social, present), sympathetic (mobilised, fight/flight), dorsal vagal (shutdown, collapse).

The crash-recompile cycle may be a dorsal vagal drop followed by ventral vagal restoration.

The polyvagal framework explains why borrowed peace works through the body — a safe, low-demand environment activates ventral vagal tone, which restores the pre-frontal cortex's capacity, which makes the inner witness audible again. Sequence: external safety → nervous system regulation → cognitive restoration → philosophical access.

## Eustress dependency

Eustress — positive, productive stress — as the trained operating fuel. The system runs on it. Not distress. Not dysfunction. A specific physiological configuration that produces high output under demand and crashes when demand is removed.

**The crash-recompile cycle:** When the fuel source is removed — leave, job transition, absence of external demand — the system doesn't rest. It crashes. The crash is real: days of low function, physical and cognitive fog, difficulty generating motivation. Then the recompile begins: the parasympathetic system rebuilds the baseline, the nervous system recalibrates, and the system comes back online with restored capacity.

**Philosophy made physiological:** The worth-machine is not only a conceptual framework — it has a biological substrate. The cortisol dependency IS the worth-machine expressed in the nervous system. Philosophy expressed as physiology.

**What it is not:** Not weakness. Not dysfunction. Not a character failing. A trained configuration — built through years of high-demand, high-output living. The system did exactly what it was trained to do. The question is whether the configuration serves the values, or whether it is running on autopilot past its useful range.

# Hormones — The Chemical Weather

Hormones are not signals in the computational sense. They are the chemical weather the whole system runs in. They don't send a message — they change the environment in which all other processing happens. This is why they are mycelium-level, not OS-level. They are the medium, not the message.

## Named hormones

**Cortisol** — the stress hormone. Primary fuel for the eustress-dependent system. Produces focus, urgency, and sustained output under demand. Chronically elevated: suppresses testosterone, impairs immune function, degrades pre-frontal cortex access over time. The most significant hormone in this system's current configuration.

**Adrenaline** — short-burst stress response. The body's emergency fuel. Distinct from cortisol — faster, shorter, sharper. The river and the snake ran on adrenaline. Grit courage does not.

**Dopamine** — the anticipation and reward hormone. The feedback loop in meaning-driven work. When work has purpose, dopamine sustains the grit-courage system. When work loses meaning — grit without signal — dopamine drops. The loop breaks. The motion continues but the fuel changes quality.

**Testosterone** — physical capability, drive, and competitive engagement. Suppressed by chronic cortisol. Relevant to the musculature system and the hero arc. Also relevant to the worth-machine: testosterone is connected to status, dominance, and self-efficacy signals — all of which interact with coherence-worth.

**Oxytocin** — the bonding and trust hormone. Released through physical touch, eye contact, and felt connection. The biological substrate of relational-worth. The system that pours enormous energy into relationships but metabolises little of it as relational-worth may be running a bonding system that doesn't complete its circuit — high oxytocin investment, low oxytocin return.

## The gap

Other hormones are significant but not yet mapped: serotonin (baseline mood and stability), DHEA (adrenal health, the counter-cortisol), growth hormone (repair and recovery, primarily nocturnal — sleep-dependent), insulin and metabolic hormones (fuel regulation, directly affecting cognitive function and energy). These are named as a gap. The blood panel is the instrument for making them legible.

## Interaction effects

The systems do not run in parallel. They regulate each other. Chronically elevated cortisol suppresses testosterone. Sleep deprivation elevates cortisol. Low testosterone reduces dopamine sensitivity. High oxytocin investment without return may elevate cortisol through unresolved social stress. The whole hormonal system is a web, not a list. This is the richest unmapped territory in the mycelium layer.

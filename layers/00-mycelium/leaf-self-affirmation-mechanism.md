# Self-Affirmation and Behavioral Activation — Mixed Empirical Grounding

"Fake it till you make it" / self-directed strategic belief adoption has real but MIXED empirical grounding, not uncritical support. Self-affirmation theory (Steele) shows genuine stress-reactivity and performance benefits under identity threat in some contexts, shrinking at scale in later multi-site replication. Behavioral activation (acting the way you want to feel, before feeling it) has decent CBT support — action shaping self-concept, not only the reverse.

**Terminology note:** J's phrase "gaslight into competence" was corrected on precision, not intent — gaslighting is deception of another against their interest. Self-directed, chosen, revocable belief-adoption for one's own benefit is closer to a knowingly self-administered placebo. The intended discomfort of the phrase was kept; the word implying harm to someone else was swapped.

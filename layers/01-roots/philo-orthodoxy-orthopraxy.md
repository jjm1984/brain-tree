# Partial Religious Adoption as the Normal Condition

Triggered by J asking: does adopting a religion mean adopting all its tenets? If not, is partial adoption adherence or rebellion, and who decides — text or the behaviour of disciples?

Partial religious adoption is not a deviant case needing justification — it is the normal condition of religion in practice. The "text vs. behaviour" question has no single answer because religious traditions themselves disagree: orthodoxy-centered traditions (historically Christianity, especially Protestant strands) locate the measure in right belief; orthopraxy-centered traditions (historically Judaism, Islam) locate it more in right communal practice and law. Every reform movement, schism, and denomination in history is structurally someone's "partial adoption" later institutionalised as its own orthodoxy.

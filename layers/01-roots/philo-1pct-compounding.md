# 1% Gains That Compound

**Small, consistent improvements compound into significant change over time. The unit of progress is not the dramatic leap but the daily anchor.**

Connects to the meaning philosophy: consistency is how intention becomes real. Connects to grit courage: compounding requires showing up without adrenaline.

## What it resists

The dramatic transformation model — the idea that change comes from a single decision, a peak experience, or a sudden shift. This philosophy holds that most lasting change is accumulated, not arrived at.

## The honest tension

This philosophy and the consistency gap sit in direct tension. The gap is not explained away by the compounding model — if anything, the compounding model makes the gap more costly. Every broken chain is a reset.

The reframe: the job change is the structural redesign window. The compounding doesn't start until the structure supports it.

## Open threads

- What does 1% look like on a bad week — does it still count?
- Is compounding a metaphor that holds in human life — or does it underestimate the role of rupture?

## Counters

**Discontinuous change** — most significant transformation is rupture, crisis, sudden shift. The 1% model may underestimate disruption.

**Quality over quantity** — one transformative act outweighs a thousand small ones. Compounding is a financial metaphor that may not hold in human life.

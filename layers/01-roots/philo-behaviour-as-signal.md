# Behaviour Is the Signal — Costly Action Over Cheap Words

If a person's words and behaviour do not match, the behaviour is the signal — refined through the session that produced this node to: costly, patterned action is the signal, not cheap or unpatterned talk. Applies across family, work, and romantic partnership. At work: verbal agreement without follow-through is the tell. In partnership: you can love someone and not act in line with it, and the behaviour is what matters — though if words are the only available channel (see the capacity thread below), consistent, effortful, specific affirmation may itself qualify as costly patterned action rather than cheap talk, since it is a deliberate routing-around of a constraint rather than a free assertion.

Raised by J as a long-held belief, developed across three exchanges in the same sitting into this Root philosophy.

## Sub-claim: pattern, not instance

Pattern, not single instance, is what determines whether a mismatch is a one-off or a trend. A single behaviour carries little evidential weight; a repeated, dateable pattern carries much more. Directly inherits entry-024's symmetry-breaking test (evidenced pattern > narrated pattern), applied here to reading OTHERS rather than reading the self.

## Crossroads: signal vs. capacity

The same observable mismatch between words and behaviour can be generated by two different causes that look identical from outside in the moment: (1) misalignment — the words were never true; or (2) a capacity gap — the words are true but execution is impaired (e.g. neurodivergence, dysregulation, depleted mycelium-layer substrate per entry-026/entry-027). J's belief was built to detect (1); raising neurodivergence as a live possibility for people close to him means a meaningful portion of what has historically read as (1) may actually be (2). Not resolved — held open as a genuine crossroads, not collapsed into either reading by default.

## Sub-claim: love is meant to be felt, not just given

Independent of cause (misalignment or capacity gap), the felt impact of a behaviour/word mismatch on the receiver does not change. Understanding the capacity-gap explanation may change CULPABILITY (how much to hold the person responsible) but does not change IMPACT (whether the mismatch still cost the receiver the felt experience of being loved). J's own words (2026-07-11): "It doesn't change how it feels to receive though. Love is meant to be felt, not just given." This decouples culpability from impact — both can be held simultaneously without one erasing the other, consistent with the tree's Tension-Holding principle.

## Amendment: affirmation as costly signal (same sitting, addendum)

Affirmation counts as costly, patterned action (not cheap talk) under two joint conditions: (1) consistency — repeated and effortful, not a one-off; and (2) additive, not substitutive — it must be layered ON TOP OF whatever action-based effort the person's actual capacity permits, not offered INSTEAD OF harder action that was genuinely available and avoided. Consistency alone establishes non-cheapness; additive-vs-substitutive establishes honesty of the signal.

J has directly witnessed this pattern (2026-07-11): people using consistent affirmation to circumvent a real constraint. Clarified same session: the circumvention observed is additive, not substitutive — i.e. it is the legitimate case (routing around a genuine capacity gap to still deliver connection), not the failure mode of swapping cheaper words for avoidable harder action. No open counter/failure-mode is needed at this time; the hypothesized substitutive failure-mode was raised by Claude as a guard-rail but is not what J has actually observed.

**Invariant, unchanged regardless of how the affirmation test resolves:** "does not undo actions" stands. Affirmation earns its own standing as signal on a separate ledger; it does not net against or retroactively reclassify a harmful or absent action. Ledgers are named separately, not pooled — consistent with the tree's existing worth-machine multi-ledger architecture.

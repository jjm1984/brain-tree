# OODA Loop — Boyd's Decision Cycle

Observe-Orient-Decide-Act as a continuous, re-entrant decision cycle, not a linear plan. Boyd's deeper philosophical claim (from his essay "Destruction and Creation", 1976): any mental model of reality eventually encounters problems it cannot solve from within itself; the only valid move at that point is to tear the model down and build a new one, not to keep refining the old one. Boyd built this synthesis explicitly from Gödel's incompleteness theorem, Heisenberg's uncertainty principle, and the Second Law of Thermodynamics (entropy/open-systems necessity).

## Convergent traditions

**Deming/Shewhart PDCA (or PDSA)** — quality management, mid-20th century. Plan-Do-Study-Act. Deming insisted on "Study" over "Check" specifically to prevent the cycle degrading into a rubber-stamp loop — directly relevant caution for `map-life-direction-cycle`'s Weekly Review stage, which risks the same hollowing-out if not genuinely interrogated each time.

**Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle** — education/psychology, 1984. Concrete Experience → Reflective Observation → Abstract Conceptualization → Active Experimentation. Real peer-reviewed debate exists directly comparing the two: Ryder, M. & Downs, C. (2022), "Rethinking reflective practice: John Boyd's OODA loop as an alternative to Kolb," *The International Journal of Management Education*, 20(3), 100703 — arguing OODA is better suited to fast-changing environments than Kolb's more static reflective model.

**Cybernetics (Norbert Wiener)** — systems theory. The general feedback-loop ancestor underlying both OODA and PDCA — named as the deeper convergence point, not a separate independent line.

**philo-1pct-compounding** (sibling root, added 2026-07-19 in a second reconciliation pass — see reference below) — small-iteration compounding and fast-decision-cycling are close cousins, both rejecting the single-dramatic-leap model of change in favour of accumulated iteration. Kaizen — already an exposed root under `philo-1pct-compounding`, not new research — belongs to this same quality-management/incremental-improvement family; OODA sits adjacent as a faster, more adversarial-context cousin of that family, not a separate lineage. `philo-1pct-compounding`'s own counters ("discontinuous change," "quality over quantity") parallel OODA's standing counter closely — both roots carry structurally similar internal tensions; this parallel is itself worth naming, not just the convergence.

## Standing counter

Real academic critique exists (surfaced in OODA-loop literature reviews): OODA is "one way among a myriad of ways of describing intuitive processes of learning and decision making that most people experience daily... not unique or especially profound" — and its popularity as a management/business-book buzzword has arguably outpaced its actual explanatory rigor in a lot of secondary usage. Held alongside the principle, not omitted.

## Crossroads — not resolved

Boyd built OODA for adversarial, competitive, time-pressured contexts (dogfights, warfare, business competition). Extending it across all of life risks importing a combat/adversarial frame into domains that aren't adversarial (self-care, relationships, family). Named as an open crossroads — do not assume universal fit.

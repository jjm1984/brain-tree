# Hold Belief Firmly — Release It When It No Longer Serves You

A second facet of the trunk, alongside 'do what is fulfilling — cause no harm' (philo-how-to-live). Not Roots content (what is believed) and not a branch — it is the operating loop by which belief earns and loses the right to be held: idea → hypothesis → tested against counters and multiple frameworks → provisional theory → action, looped, never closed.

## The ring mechanism

**Serves means function, not comfort.** Serving means function, honesty, growth — not ease. A hard, unmovable, uncomfortable truth is not automatically failing to serve. Discomfort and service are not opposites; this guards against the line becoming a permission structure for dropping true things because they are heavy.

**Two cases under one word.** This facet deliberately covers two cases: (1) a compiled/inherited belief that has genuinely outlived its function (dropped without needing to be argued down); and (2) a TRUE belief that has become captivity — held past its use not because it's false but because it has calcified into autopilot rather than active, chosen carrying (the raft case).

## Corroboration

**Tier 1 — originating, independent:** William James's pragmatism (cash-value test); the Buddhist raft parable (a true teaching set down once it has served crossing).

**Tier 2 — independent, adjacent:** Wittgenstein's ladder (Tractatus 6.54) — discarded after use.

**Tier 3 — derivative, but the real trigger:** Derek Sivers's *Useful Not True* — contemporary pragmatism restated, kept honestly in provenance as the actual proximate trigger of this line of thinking, not treated as independent corroborating weight on its own. (An earlier draft had misattributed this idea to Thomas Mann via a Nietzsche/Vaihinger lineage — corrected once J identified Sivers as the direct source.)

## Standing counters (permanent, not just logged once)

Nietzsche — convictions are more dangerous enemies of truth than lies. Thomas Mann — "a harmful truth is better than a useful lie," explicitly arguing the opposite hierarchy. Both must remain permanently attached here as the check against 'serves' sliding into 'convenient.'

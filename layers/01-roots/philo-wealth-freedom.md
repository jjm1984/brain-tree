# Wealth as Freedom From the Rat Race

## The confluence of dissatisfaction (2025-12-26)

A recurring dissatisfaction traced to the rat race: working for a wage, buying in the city, inability to build wealth directly, slavery to inflation. Christmas and commercialisation make the fragility of this arrangement poignant. Counter-claim held alongside it: "we can make happiness, our labour doesn't inflate like goods and it's not taxed" — naming meat, vegetables, raw foods, milk, alcohol, leisure time, and music as things labour can produce directly. Also: a home large enough to share without imposing.

## Three aphoristic fragments (2025)

"Choosing the best option at every turn won't get you where you want to be." Media is designed to trigger hormonal satisfaction so you never pursue the adventure or the big leap. "All your life you are taught moderation but a moderate man never made it up Everest."

## Little Brains (2025)

Worry is the brain running scenarios. Less use of the limbic brain now than 100 years ago — a safer society. State school was designed to produce workers for industrialisation, not risk-reward capitalism. To get out of the rat race you have to teach risk-reward thinking.

## Paying ourselves first (2024-03-03)

"Paying ourselves first" — Proverbs 24:27. Poor people buy things; middle class save to spend on housing and education; wealthy people invest.

## Counter: Jordan Peterson's Four Pillars (2024-03-03)

Jordan Peterson's Four Pillars of a Meaningful Life: Belonging, Purpose, Transcendence, Storytelling. A purely financial escape-the-rat-race framing is cut against by a meaning-framework that doesn't centre money at all.

## Priorities (2023-11-30)

Give kids a good start so they don't have to battle through; pass on generational wealth and knowledge; provide a place to anchor to; prep the family for harder times; recession-proof finances and future.

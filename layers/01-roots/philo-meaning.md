# The Philosophy of Meaning

**Meaning is discovered through doing. It lives in the intention, the integrity of the motion, and how it ripples through the world. But intention without follow-through is an empty promise. There is real responsibility for the outcome — not total ownership, but genuine accountability.**

## How it was built

Built sentence by sentence in one conversation — not adopted from a tradition. First: 'Meaning is discovered through doing — identity follows action. Meaning is also proven to have value through the action and the outcome.' Then pressed on failed outcomes. Refinement: 'It's the intention and the pushing and the way that it moves through the world that matters.' Then the correction: 'Intention without follow-through is a dream. It's an empty promise to yourself.'

## The original contribution

The phrase 'how it moves through the world' gives meaning a ripple quality — it doesn't just live in the actor or the outcome, but in what the action sets in motion. Almost ecological. This is the original contribution — none of the traditions quite capture it. The Stoics release the outcome entirely. The existentialists invent rather than discover meaning. The pragmatists test through consequence but don't give meaning this ripple texture.

## The key distinctions

- Distinct from **pure Stoicism**: which releases the outcome. This position refuses that release — outcomes are partially yours.
- Distinct from **pure existentialism**: which invents rather than discovers meaning. This position holds that there is something to find.
- Distinct from **Pragmatism**: which tests through consequence but centres on utility, not on the ripple of action through the world.

## Open threads

- Can meaning be discovered through stillness as well as doing — or is action always required?
- What happens to meaning when the follow-through is there but the outcome still fails — despite everything?
- Can meaning be discovered through suffering and loss — not just doing?

## Counters

**Nihilism** — meaning doesn't exist. The motion ripples into nothing. Camus called this the absurd — and didn't flinch from it.

**Pure Stoicism** — outcome is irrelevant to the quality of the act. Your position holds outcome as partially yours — Stoicism refuses that.

**Existentialism (Sartre)** — meaning isn't discovered, it's invented. There's nothing to find. You impose it.

## Exposed roots

- **Pragmatism (James, Dewey)** — meaning proves itself through action and consequence. Truth is tested, not declared. `yellow resonant`
- **Aristotle — Eudaimonia** — the archer who draws perfectly in a storm and misses — the excellence was real. `yellow partially landed`
- **Bhagavad Gita — action without attachment to fruit** — do your duty without ownership of the outcome. `peripheral`
- **Virtue Ethics (Aristotle, MacIntyre)** — the triad (happy, strong, healthy) is virtue ethics in practice without having named it that. `yellow present unnamed`

## Dear reader

The phrase 'how it moves through the world' is the place where this philosophy departs from everything it sits near. The reader who finds this resonant will know why — there is a difference between what you do and what it becomes after you have done it. The second belongs to the world. But you are still answerable for it.

# What People Are For

**People are for connection and shared meaning — but most of all, to be understood by others.**

This is a root statement. It has a quiet ache in it. The longing to be understood is rarely abstract.

Connection and shared meaning is the social architecture. 'Most of all to be understood' is the deepest need in the hierarchy — not just to connect, but to be seen accurately as the full thing you are.

## The tree itself

The tree is simultaneously a tool for self-understanding and a record that could allow someone else to understand you fully. Building it may be an act of the thing you believe most deeply. The 'dear reader' moment: writing for an audience is an act of wanting to be understood. If I make my thinking legible, maybe I can be understood.

## The honest state

Held lightly but honestly: does not feel fully understood by those closest. Not a wound — not nothing. A true thing, named without drama.

## The tension

The two root philosophies pull in opposite directions — inward fulfilment (How to Live Well) and outward understanding (What People Are For). This tension is not a flaw. It is generative. Confirmed when asked: it is absolutely both.

## Open threads

- Is the act of building this tree partly a desire to be understood — by myself, or by others?
- Does being understood require being fully known — or can partial understanding be enough?

## Counters

**Existentialism (Sartre)** — people are fundamentally alone. The desire to be understood is bad faith — seeking refuge in the other's gaze.

**Nietzsche** — the will to power, not the need for understanding, is the primary drive. Connection as need signals weakness.

**Buddhism** — the self that wants to be understood is an illusion. Attachment to being known is ego-clinging.

## Exposed roots

- **Buber — I-Thou** — being met as a subject, not an object. The hunger to be met as the full thing you are, not a version someone has constructed of you. `yellow resonant`
- **Relational philosophy (Ubuntu, care ethics)** — the self constituted through relation. Meaning made together. `peripheral`

# People Before Process (working title)

When people and process compete for attention or resources, people take priority — not as a slogan but as a lived, consistent decision rule across contexts. The chain of consequence runs people → customers → revenue/outcomes; optimising the end of that chain directly is treated as weaker than tending its start.

Surfaced directly from J's own dictated words, 2026-07-05 — not inferred from external documents like the earlier work-vocation role index. Named explicitly as a North Star spanning J's career: civilian leadership style, how he operates within high-performing military teams, and one-on-one customer/farmer/dealer interactions all show the same ordering.

## Tension holding

Held open rather than resolved: is this a general ethical stance about the priority of people, or a narrower pattern about responding to need/dependency specifically? The lived evidence supports the narrower reading; the way J described it in his own words supports the broader one.

## Further reading

Servant leadership, Jobs-to-be-done, and Aristotle's Rhetoric are named above as speculative or partial resonances — none confirmed as sources J has directly engaged, except Sinek's Golden Circle, which has a traceable prior engagement in J's own materials.

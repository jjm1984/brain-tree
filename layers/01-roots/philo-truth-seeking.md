# Fallibilist Realism — Truth Tested, Not Declared

Triggered by the Moore dialogic-humility thread. J explicitly rejects relativism ('not just your truth') and absolutism (no claim to a finished, fully-knowable Truth) simultaneously. The tree is embodied action for this stance: surface a belief, communicate it, receive counters, test it against multiple independent frameworks, and what survives is "not absolute but truth tested... found to hold ground — something becoming truer than where I started."

This is the philosophical justification underneath the tree's own structural principles (Counters, Crossroads, Dear Reader) — they exist because this is how J believes truth is actually approached.

## Three axes for weighing corroborating sources (2026-07-09)

Surfaced while grounding trunk-belief-release-when-no-longer-serves: J caught an unintended chronological-primacy bias in how sources were being weighted (older treated as more foundational than modern). Correcting the framing separated three distinct axes that had been collapsed into one:

- **Instrument diversity (legitimate):** different epistemic instruments (analytic philosophy, religion/tradition, contemporary popularization) corroborating independently is real evidential weight — not their age.
- **Time-testedness (legitimate, under this philosophy's own Popperian framework):** more time generally means more accumulated opportunities to have been refuted and wasn't. This is a fact about testing-rounds survived, not a virtue of age itself.
- **Chronological primacy — who said it first (illegitimate, explicitly rejected):** J does not want this treated as load-bearing. A modern, independently-arrived-at instance of a position stands as its own corroboration, not a derivative shadow of an older 'real' source.

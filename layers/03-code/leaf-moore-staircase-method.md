# Geoffrey Moore's "Infinite Staircase" — Structural Method vs. Content Authority

J follows Moore closely for tech-adoption work (*Crossing the Chasm*, the Technology Adoption Life Cycle). Moore has since written *The Infinite Staircase* (2021), a metaphysics/ethics book building an emergentist ladder from physics → chemistry → biology → social science → humanities → ethics. J raised his own suspicion unprompted: is he over-weighting Moore's metaphysical claims because Moore is a trusted mentor in an unrelated domain?

**Named bias: authority-halo / trust-transfer bias** — crediting a person's claims in an unproven domain because of their track record in a proven, unrelated domain. J self-identified this directly and unprompted.

## Content claims evaluated on their own merits (zero inherited authority)

**Morality prior to religion (supported, weak version only).** J agrees religion transmits and formalises pre-existing moral sense; it does not originate it. Explicitly rejects the strong version (religion as fully reducible epiphenomenon of biology), which would have broken domain-relative authority for the religion instrument. J's own example: Cain killing Abel was morally wrong before Abraham founded religious worship or his sons' paths diverged into Judaism/Islam.

J later confirmed the Cain-and-Abel illustration was a deliberate translator move, not naive self-reinforcement: using language and instrument (myth/religion) native to the audience to land a claim that might otherwise meet resistance — a direct match to the already-grounded translator-role skill-node (cross-role-foundations). Counter: deliberateness resolves the rhetorical-strategy question but not the epistemic one — choosing the religious frame makes the claim land better with a religious audience; it does not make the underlying claim (morality preceding religion) any more or less true. The argument's actual force rests on the Cain-and-Abel logic itself, independent of the vocabulary used to deliver it. Persuasion and evidential support are kept as separate axes here, even though this one illustration serves both.

**Orthodoxy vs. orthopraxy in religious adoption** — split out into its own Roots-layer candidate, see philo-orthodoxy-orthopraxy (forming). Triggered in the same session by J asking whether adopting a religion means adopting all its tenets.

**Content claim 3 — downgraded to heuristic, not value architecture.** Discussed and explicitly downgraded by J to a heuristic checkpoint rather than a value structure. Flagged in the source session as needing a write-back check to confirm the downgrade is reflected here rather than only in conversational record — logged as such; the specific content of claim 3 itself was not captured in enough detail in the available session record to state further without guessing. Open thread: revisit with J to capture what claim 3 actually was and confirm the downgrade explicitly.

## Two structural extensions (tension-held, not grounded — 2026-07-09)

Per J's explicit instruction on commit: these are held as **tension-held**, not "grounded" or "yellow" — the point is for the reader to see the challenge held live against the structure, not resolved in the tree's favour. J can keep using the staircase-as-method in the Code layer while the metaphysical question underneath it stays open.

**Emergent irreducibility.** Moore's actual mechanism (not just his structure): each rung of the staircase is causally irreducible to the one below it — chemistry isn't "just" physics, biology isn't "just" chemistry. If true, this gives the tree's five-instrument non-privileging a stronger philosophical grounding than pragmatic division of labour: the instruments would capture genuinely non-reducible strata of reality, not just useful lenses. Counter: emergentism is contested, not settled. Reductionists (Dennett and others) argue apparent irreducibility is epistemic, not ontological — we can't currently reduce biology to physics, which isn't the same as a categorical impossibility. If reductionism is right, the five-instrument framework loses this support and falls back to "useful division of labour" rather than "reflects reality's structure." Resolution: held open. Landing posture is useful-not-true — J keeps the five-instrument methodology regardless of which metaphysical picture is correct, because the framework's value is in what it does (prevents any one instrument overreaching into another's jurisdiction), not in whether emergentism or reductionism is the deeper truth.

**Is-ought discontinuity at the Code layer.** Moore treats ethics as a rung that cannot be derived from the physical rungs below it — it must emerge as genuinely new, not be squeezed out of "is" statements (Hume's guillotine). This sharpens the reason the tree's Code layer stays deferred: not only "wait until the tree is rich enough" but "premature construction risks smuggling an ought out of an is" — the same failure mode Moore is trying to avoid at his ethics rung. Counter: this borrows Moore's justificatory structure, not just his staircase shape — a subtler form of the same trust-transfer risk already logged against him. The published critique that his emergentism is more a description of multiple valid framings than an actual mechanism applies here too: "ethics emerges, therefore don't force it" could be an evasion of the is-ought problem rather than a real solution to it. Resolution: held open. The deferred-Code-layer decision was already made independently on its own grounds (partial code has dangerous blind spots) — this tension adds a second possible justification without the tree needing to depend on it being correct. Useful-not-true applies here as well.

## Self-worth calibration (Map layer, not a structural change)

Not a change to the three-ledger self-worth framework (coherence/validation/legacy-worth), which stays intact and unweighted for the reader. J's own personal calibration on top of it: legacy-worth carries the most weight in strategic contexts; coherence-worth carries the most weight in everyday action; validation-worth has rarely helped J directly, except where it folds toward self-validation, which J experiences as close to coherence-worth. Resolved on clarification: self-validation is explicitly a measurement tool used to gauge coherence-worth, not a separate value ledger running parallel to it — they are not two axes at all; self-validation is instrumentation, coherence-worth is the thing being measured. J also named a tentative, hedged cause for why validation-by-others has never carried much weight: early isolation — offered as "likely," not settled fact, and logged as lived-experience with that hedge preserved, not to be treated as diagnostic or built on further unless J raises it again.

J's own words: "A three legged stool can still balance well, and I think it is for the reader to decide. For me, legacy is important in strategy, coherence in everyday action, and validation worth has never helped me much unless you count self validation which is close to coherence." And: "validation by others hasn't really hit home for me, likely due to the early isolation."

## Second independent instance: the staircase logic in nervous-system communication (2026-07-18)

Moore's staircase names a structural rule — each rung requires the one below it to be accessible before the next is reachable — previously applied in this tree only to metaphysics/epistemology. The co-regulation material (`leaf-borrowed-peace-co-regulation`, `mycelium-brain`) is a second, independent instance of the *same* structural principle operating in the biology of communication: nervous-system regulation (the lower rung) must be accessible before verbal/philosophical repair (the higher rung) is reachable at all — already implicit in `leaf-borrowed-peace`'s own sequence (external safety → nervous system regulation → cognitive restoration → philosophical access), now explicitly named as the same staircase logic, not a coincidental resemblance. J's own framing, direct quote basis: "the accessible modes of communication are hierarchical" — nervous system and body language are accessible before language.

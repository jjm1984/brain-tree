# How the Tree Holds Knowledge — Five Instruments

The tree holds five kinds of knowing simultaneously. Each has a domain and a limit.

**Jurisdictional authority (resolved wording, 2026-07-11 — replaces the earlier "none is privileged" phrasing entirely, not held alongside it):** Each epistemic instrument holds near-total authority within its own jurisdiction and near-none outside it — science on mechanism, philosophy on values/coherence, lived experience on the felt particular, myth on pre-verbal pattern, religion on compressed wisdom and meaning. They do not vote on each other's questions — each instrument owns its question outright. Standing separately: the raw occurrence — event or felt experience — has priority over every instrument's account of it (priority of the given). All interpretation is downstream of something that did not require interpretation to be real. Tested against: the cortisol/meaning/felt-choice example; grief; the 2022 misery; the river (age 6); flood-as-religious-cleansing vs flood-as-archaeological-event ("myth-form needs no proof, archaeology doesn't need religion — ownership of meaning transfers, not truth-value"). Term correction: "ways of knowing" / "epistemic instruments" is the correct term; "schools" was wrong — a school is a position within a domain (e.g. Stoicism within philosophy), not a domain itself.

| Instrument | Best for | Limit | Tag |
|------------|----------|-------|-----|
| **Philosophy** | Values, coherence, meaning frameworks | Can build consistent systems wrong about the world | `philosophy` |
| **Science & medicine** | Body mechanisms, interventions, the mycelium layer | Can only speak to what is measurable | `science` |
| **Lived experience** | The specific, irreducible, particular | Not generalisable; subject to narrative distortion | `lived-experience` |
| **Myth & culture** | Making the pre-verbal legible; felt-but-not-proven | Resonance can confirm bias; not evidence | `myth-form` |
| **Religion & tradition** | Compressed wisdom; pre-rational knowing; ritual | Becomes dogma when held as the only instrument | `religion` |

The tensions between these instruments are not problems to resolve. They are the most honest thing the tree can model. Holding religion, science, and lived experience simultaneously — in apparent contradiction — is intellectual honesty about the limits of each lens.

## Tension holding as stance

The five instruments will sometimes contradict each other. A neuroscience finding may challenge a philosophical position. A lived experience may resist what the science says. A myth may carry something true that no instrument can yet verify.

When this happens, the tree names it and leaves it open. The instrument tag on each node identifies which kind of knowing is speaking. The reader can see what is grounding a claim and judge accordingly.

This is not relativism. Some instruments are better suited to some questions. A philosophical claim about what makes life meaningful is not undermined by the fact that neuroscience cannot measure meaning. A scientific finding about cortisol dynamics is not negated by a philosophical commitment to agency. Both can be true, in their domain, simultaneously.

### Worked example: instrument-boundary overreach (2026-07-11)

Peterson's claim that humans are "information foragers, evolved to live on the border between explored and unexplored territory... where the meaning that buttresses us against tragedy is found" blends two different things without marking the seam: (1) legitimate trait science — Openness to Experience is a real, measured DIMENSION OF INDIVIDUAL VARIATION (some people score high, some low — his own model's Orderliness pole describes people who find genuine meaning through routine and predictability, not chaos-confrontation); and (2) a species-universal mythological claim about where ALL humans find meaning. Individual-difference data cannot license a universal claim without additional argument — this is the overreach. The legitimate underlying science is closer to exploration-exploitation tradeoffs in behavioral ecology, a species/strategy-level literature distinct from trait-level individual-difference data. This is science lending unearned authority to a myth-form claim without marking where the science stops and the myth begins. `epistemic tag: philosophy (analysis of instrument-boundary crossing)`

### Myth-form instrument row — supporting and undermining evidence (2026-07-11, consolidated Maps of Meaning package)

**Supports the instrument boundary as drawn:** Independent scholarly uptake beyond Peterson's own argument — Hood, Hill & Spilka's *The Psychology of Religion: An Empirical Approach* (2009) cites *Maps of Meaning* as a serious contribution to the psychology of religion, exploring the tension between tradition and transformation as the personality basis for belief architecture. `yellow resonant` Also: Peterson's own claim that myths encode accumulated adaptive behavioral wisdom, transmitted because functional rather than as primitive proto-science, strengthens the myth-form definition with a functional mechanism rather than only "resonance, felt-but-not-proven." `yellow resonant`

**Undermines / confirms the limit as drawn:** *Maps of Meaning* relies heavily on mythological pattern-matching with comparatively little historical or sociological evidence underneath it — empirical thinness that is exactly myth-form's known limit per this table, not a new problem, a confirmation of the existing instrument boundary. `yellow, self-confirming rather than novel` Separately, two concrete counters flagged against the book's "compatible with modern science" framing, held open rather than resolved in the book's favour: philosopher Paul Thagard's 2018 review (*Psychology Today*) called the book murky and argued it fails as anthropology, psychology, philosophy, and politics; and Peterson's discussion of Gödel's Incompleteness Theorem in the book has been identified as a factual misreading of what the theorem proves, not a simplification — a specific, checkable technical error. `yellow, counter, concrete`

Possibly self-undermining, possibly an unexamined blind spot, flagged as a structural observation rather than a settled claim. ## Deferred Truth

A third epistemic move, distinct from (a) the Trunk's belief-earn/lose loop (sincere belief, actively tested against evidence) and (b) Derek Sivers' "useful not true" (no truth-commitment from the start, no expectation of ever revisiting). Deferred truth: a sincerely-doubted account, consciously narrowed in scope and postponed rather than resolved, carrying an implicit promise to return to it.

Tested against J's 2013 lived case: "this hardship is temporary" was a scope-narrowed version of a fuller, suspected truth ("the direction of my life is wrong"), held through genuine crisis (marriage, newborn) without active testing. Structural risk named: deferred truths don't self-terminate the way Sivers' fictions don't need to (never promised resolution) or Trunk-line belief do (actively tested) — without a forcing mechanism, "later" can silently become "never" once the circumstance that justified deferral resolves. Nine years (2013–2022) is the lived example.

**Open design question, not yet answered:** what mechanism would force a deferred truth's expiration deliberately, next time.

## Transmutability of the Given

Completes jurisdictional supremacy (a synchronic claim — who has authority right now) with a diachronic/simultaneous claim: a single event or idea can pass through multiple epistemic instruments over time, each fully re-describing it in its own terms without needing the others' permission, and can arrive back at its origin instrument transformed by having passed through the others.

**Two confirmed modes:**

- **Sequential** — an event moves through instruments one after another and returns to its origin instrument changed: myth-form ("every hero needs a dragon") → philosophy (essence of cause, Trunk tension) → science (cortisol/testosterone mechanism) → lived experience (the specific formative moments, years of training) → myth-form again (examined and conscious this time, rather than simply inherited). A spiral, not a circle — the same category returned to at a different altitude.
- **Simultaneous** — several instruments occupy the same event AT ONCE rather than in sequence, still under jurisdictional supremacy, just running in parallel. Illness and healing is the clearest case: science, philosophy, religion, myth, and lived experience can all speak to the same event at once. Unexplained recovery is not myth OVERRULING science — it is myth/religion supplying meaning for a gap science honestly hasn't yet closed.

**Caution, to be preserved wherever this principle is applied:** "the body heals through positive self-view" sits on a real boundary. Bounded, legitimate science exists here (psychoneuroimmunology, stress-immune interaction, placebo response) but it is specific and limited, not a general mechanism for reversing serious illness through belief. Conflating the philosophical claim (meaning shapes how illness is lived) with an unsupported medical claim (belief reliably cures disease) is exactly the kind of drift transmutability could cause if not held carefully.

## Further reading

The epistemology of instrument-holding connects to: Thomas Kuhn on paradigm shifts, Michael Polanyi on tacit knowledge, William James on radical empiricism, and the phenomenological tradition (Husserl, Heidegger, Merleau-Ponty) on the limits of third-person accounts of first-person experience. None of these are currently in the tree. They are named here as the door.

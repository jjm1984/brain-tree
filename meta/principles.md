# Tree Structure Principles

These are the architectural rules the tree holds itself to. Not guidelines — operating constraints.

## Crossroads

When two ideas within the tree pull against each other without resolution — or when a philosophy could genuinely develop in more than one direction — this is marked as a CROSSROADS. Both paths are named. Neither is closed. The choosing is left visible.

A crossroads is not a flaw or a contradiction. It is a decision point where the direction taken reveals something about who you are. The reader is invited to draw their own conclusion.

## Counters

Every philosophy carries the traditions and positions that challenge or contradict it. A philosophy without its counters is not fully formed — it is a preference that has not been tested. Knowing what you push against is part of knowing what you believe.

Counters are held with equal weight to supporting ideas. They are named, not dismissed.

## Exposed Roots

Traditions that the philosophy sits near, resonates with, or was unknowingly shaped by. Marked honestly: some resonant, some peripheral, some unengaged. None claimed as sources unless explicitly adopted.

**Alignment-Frequency Is Not Engagement-Depth** (methodology addendum, 2026-07-11): A source accumulating multiple independent exposed-root citations across separate nodes — because it happens to say several things that resonate — does not by itself earn elevated status (e.g. "virtual mentor," a dedicated Knowledge branch). Elevated status is earned by sustained engagement with a source's whole framework — tested against counters, held in tension deliberately, worked across multiple sessions (per the standard already set by Geoffrey Moore's placement, see leaf-moore-staircase-structural-method). Scattered resonant citations stay exactly where they land, node by node, with no umbrella entity created to bundle them. Each future citation from the same source is evaluated on its own merits, not fast-tracked because the source already has prior hits. (Trigger case: Jordan Peterson — three scattered citations landed across separate nodes; J corrected an over-claim toward "virtual mentor" status with "I enjoy finding alignment with other ideas but won't claim more than is practical.")

**Historical Coherence Is a Filtered Artifact, Not Observed Pattern** (methodology addendum, 2026-07-11): A historical figure's apparent consistency between stated ideas and lived behaviour is not directly observed — it survives through multiple layers of filtering: (1) survivorship/curation (destroyed letters, selectively published diaries, biographers' choices — inconsistencies are disproportionately lost, not disproportionately preserved), and (2) successive cultural retelling — every generation that has described the figure between their death and now has re-filtered them through its own lens, values, and agenda, compounding the distortion further from the original with each retelling. The ideas that "outlast" a person are precisely the ones robust enough to survive this compounding filter, which manufactures an appearance of coherence that was never actually verified — only never contradicted, because the contradicting evidence didn't survive the filter. A contemporary figure, by contrast, gives an unfiltered, uncurated, still-live pattern — every inconsistency visible in real time. Comparing historical-figure coherence to contemporary-figure coherence is therefore not a fair "more consistent vs less consistent" comparison — it is a comparison of curated-and-repeatedly-refiltered legacy against raw, unfiltered present. This is a distinct axis from evidentiary tiering by intellectual dependency (see leaf-moore-staircase-structural-method's trust-transfer note) — it sorts by reliability of the *source's personal coherence*, not by how dependent a claim is on the source's authority. It is also a separate risk from the genetic fallacy (an idea's truth does not depend on its source's personal coherence, in either direction) — correcting for one should not be read as softening the other. J's own words: "we romanticize historical figures and conveniently gloss over the inconsistencies because they're harder to prove... surviving historical references are filtered through the lens of culture and all who have described it to this point in time."

## Dear Reader

Where thinking is incomplete, original, or genuinely forked — a note is left for the reader. Space is held for them to dig deeper and form their own conclusions. The tree is a thinking partner, not an authority.

**Organic Audience Concentration** (caution, 2026-07-11): Distinct from the tree's already-grounded DELIBERATE translator mechanism (consciously choosing language/instrument native to a specific audience to help a claim land — see leaf-ability-translator-role, and the Cain-and-Abel move in leaf-moore-staircase-structural-method), a second, ORGANIC mechanism can produce audience concentration with zero targeting at all: certain source material has pre-existing conceptual furniture that a specific readership already owns, so the fit happens on contact. Named against Jordan Peterson's readership (religious, Stoic-adjacent, right-leaning) as plausibly organic growth, not deliberate targeting — J: "I'm not sure that he actually targets them. He just lands better there and it has organically grown." Self-applied caution: this tree's own source palette (Stoicism, warrior ethics, Bushido, Roman officium, capacity-and-hardship framing, philo-orthodoxy-orthopraxy) is structurally similar to the palette that organically concentrated Peterson's readership. The Dear Reader architecture is built for a general, undetermined future reader — organic concentration could narrow that in practice even with zero deliberate targeting anywhere in the sourcing process. Held as a watch-item, not a problem requiring correction — correcting for it by force (deliberately diversifying sources to counteract drift) would itself be a form of targeting, just aimed at a different outcome. Full treatment: leaf-ability-translator-role.md.

## Tension Holding

Where religion, science, philosophy, myth, and lived experience pull in genuinely different directions without resolution, this is named and left visible. Holding tension without forcing synthesis is a philosophical stance, not an evasion.

## Further Reading

Every branch knows its own edges. The further reading section names what lies beyond the tree's current reach. The tree is a map, not a destination. The further reading is the door. What is beyond it belongs to the reader.

## Further Thought

Paired with Further Reading, but the opposite direction. Further Reading points outward, to sources beyond the tree's current reach. Further Thought points inward: ideas generated inside the tree's own reasoning that are explicitly NOT adopted, NOT assigned a philosophy-state (not even Forming), and not a citation to go read elsewhere. A Further Thought entry marks something as worthy of dedicated future investigation, distinct from: a Crossroads (two live paths already named and held), a Tension (two instruments already pulling against each other), and an ordinary open-thread inside a feedback-loop entry (which usually has clearer next steps already visible). A Further Thought is often flagged specifically because it sits outside current expertise or requires dedicated time/attention the current session cannot give it. It is a parking spot with a name, not a diagnosis or a commitment to develop it on any timeline.

**Distinguishing test:** Ask — is there already a clear next step or a clear two-sided tension? If yes, it's a Crossroads or Tension, already-existing mechanisms. If it's genuinely just "this is interesting and I don't yet know what to do with it, and it deserves real time I don't have right now" — that's Further Thought. The existing loose `explore-further` feedback-loop entry type routes into this principle going forward; past `explore-further` entries are not retroactively relabelled (backlog item, not urgent).

**Required field:** Every Further Thought entry must carry a `domain-expertise-needed` field naming the SPECIFIC field(s) of real external expertise the idea would need reviewed by — not a generic tag like "philosophy," but specific enough to identify why a given specialist would be the right reviewer (e.g. "comparative mythology / history of religions," "philosophy of science — demarcation problem specialists," "personality psychology — trait researchers," "creativity research — computational/cognitive creativity studies," "history of ideas / intellectual history").

**Standing acknowledgment — the closed-loop limitation.** Claude's internal pressure-testing within a chat, however genuine and rigorous, does not discharge the need for real external domain review. J and Claude working alone are a closed loop — Claude's counters and challenges are broad and structural, drawn from general knowledge and pattern-matching across the tree's existing content, not from career-level specialist grounding in any single field the tree touches (theology, comparative mythology, philosophy of science, personality psychology, etc). This is named explicitly as a limitation of the tree-building process itself, not just a property of individual Further Thought entries — it applies retroactively to a meaningful portion of what is already grounded in the tree, not only to future entries. J's own words: "I would love for further thought to be shared amongst other philosophers and tested, peer reviewed. But not just other philosophers, those with real domain knowledge and expertise, because circular thought within yourself, it confirms and creates blind spots because your own knowledge is only... it's only reaching so far."

**Why the loop is structural, not just under-effort.** J named precisely why the closed-loop blind spot is structural: "we are using existing knowledge to define future ideas." Claude's internal pressure-testing, however rigorous, is combinatorial — it recombines and cross-references material already present in the tree and in Claude's training, but cannot generate what a real domain expert provides, which is not MORE of the same kind of material but a DIFFERENT KIND: lived contact with a field's actual open problems, knowledge of which "obvious" connections have already been tried and quietly failed, and the ability to identify when a question itself is the wrong question rather than just answering the question asked. This is a kind-of-knowledge gap, not a depth-of-knowledge gap — no amount of additional recombination inside the J-Claude loop closes it, which is what makes the blind spot structural rather than a solvable-with-more-effort problem.

The actual mechanism for surfacing Further Thought entries (and potentially other tree content) to genuine external domain experts for real review is not solved and stays an open practical question — logged as a backlog item.

**First logged example (2026-07-11):** *Can the testing/proving mechanism (Popper falsifiability migration, see phil-truth-seeking) apply to theology and myth's actual content, not just to empirical claims embedded within them?* Raised directly against the philosophy-science migration principle (embedded empirical claims migrate into science's jurisdiction once tested; the symbolic wrapper is discarded). The further question is whether the mechanism could go further: test/prove the felt-meaning, numinous, or moral-weight content of theology and myth themselves, not just factual claims they happen to carry. This appears to run directly against the already-grounded epistemology table (myth-form: "felt-but-not-proven"; religion: "compressed wisdom, pre-rational knowing") and the is-ought boundary used elsewhere — but J explicitly flagged this as outside current expertise/capacity to resolve now, and worthy of real future investigation rather than a quick answer. `domain-expertise-needed: philosophy of religion / theology — testability and demarcation specialists`

## Everything goes in

Add everything — settled or not, coherent or not. Honest state labelling over false coherence. The Code layer must not be built prematurely — a partial code looks complete but has dangerous blind spots.

## The feedback loop is structural

The feedback loop is not supplementary to the tree. It is the mechanism by which the tree grows honestly. A tree that is never used against reality is a collection of preferences, not a philosophy. Gaps and contradictions appear not in the building phase but in the deployment phase.

## Peripheral leaves matter as much as green ones

The gap is named. The knowledge is not yet there. That is as valuable as a settled leaf — it shows where the roots have not yet reached.

## Structural Integrity Over Interesting

An explicit corrective to human/natural tendency to follow whatever is interesting, new, or emotionally engaging when building the tree. Structural/foundational items (epistemology wording, schema, unglamorous gaps) deserve the same rigor and attention as content that is naturally engaging. Distinguished from "interesting-because-alive" (charged because genuinely load-bearing right now, e.g. grief) vs "interesting-because-easy" (bright-and-shiny, low resistance, flatters existing narrative) — the corrective targets the second, not the first. Confirmed as a standing working principle, 2026-07-11.

## Map-Layer Exception Principle

A refinement of the Map-depends-on-Code rule stated in `layers/04-map/index.md`. Some Map content is a tested, lived, already-functional tool — a practice that works and has been used, not a deduction from a Code layer that does not yet exist. Defining something as Map-layer requires testing it against Code over time as Code emerges; it is not a precondition for Map content being generated in the first place. Confirmed by J, 2026-07-11, applied to writing the seven-step relational repair pattern and the financial-reserve two-tier practice directly into the Map layer ahead of Code crystallising.

## Skills Are a Map-Layer Sub-Type, Not a 7th Layer

Resolved 2026-07-18 (J), extending the Map-Layer Exception Principle above rather than sitting beside it as a separate structure. The full chain, J's own formulation:

**Leaf** (knowledge — what's believed/known) → **Fruit + Code injection** (embodied values/morals/ideals/character, conscious or autonomic — action that should be done) → **Map** (strategy — what I plan to do) → **Skill** (tactical action executed, decomposed FROM a Map strategy node).

A Skill is not a parallel concept to Map content, competing for layer status — it is the tactical decomposition one level *below* a Map/strategy node, the same relationship a leaf has to its parent branch. This is a distinct concept from the `leaf-ability-*` nodes (renamed 2026-07-18 from `leaf-skill-*` specifically to free this term) — those are narrative, voice-sampled, tacit-judgment capability leaves meant to be read and understood; Skills are short, actionable, in-the-moment instruments meant to be reached for, not read for understanding. See `skills/SKILL-CREATION.md` for the authoring spec.

**ID prefix:** `skill-*`.

**Schema addition:** Skill nodes carry an optional `parent_map` field pointing to the Map/strategy node they tactically decompose — analogous to a leaf's `parent-branch` field.

**Provisional placement rule:** a Skill MAY be placed without a resolved `parent_map` if its natural parent Map node does not yet exist. In that case, set `parent_map: null` and add `parent_map_status: orphan-pending`, naming the anticipated future parent by name in a note. This exists specifically to avoid the "deferred truth" failure mode (later silently becoming never) — orphaned skills stay visible and queryable, not stalled indefinitely behind an unbuilt Map layer.

**Physical placement:** since Skills are a Map-layer sub-type, skill nodes live under `layers/04-map/skills/[skill-id]/`, alongside the existing skills built before this decision — not as a sibling top-level directory. `skills/SKILL-CREATION.md` (repo root) remains the authoring spec/meta-doc itself, a separate concern from where built skill instances live; this split is a known, not-yet-resolved wrinkle, see `CLAUDE.md`'s "Known inconsistency" note.

## Map Layer, Refined: The Map Buys You Freedom

J reframed the Map layer's purpose 2026-07-19, beyond the generic "daily practice; depends on Code existing first" description: Map is specifically the layer that builds capability and resources so life isn't constrained by survival-mode — **the map buys you freedom.** What you then orient a free life toward (passions, strategy) is a separate, later question from whether Map has actually built the capability to choose it. This doesn't replace the Map-Layer Exception Principle above — it explains *why* that exception exists: tested, lived tools that build capability don't need to wait on Code, because the capability-building function is prior to and separate from the philosophical grounding question.

**Existing tree connection:** this directly connects to already-confirmed `philo-wealth-freedom` content — the two-tier financial reserve structure (emergency fund absorbs singular shocks; crisis buffer buys optionality, see `layers/04-map/index.md` § "Practice: the financial reserve") is a concrete, already-placed instance of exactly this Map-layer function. Surfaced as a connection, not new content.

**Worth-machine caution, not assumed safe by default:** resource-building-as-the-real-goal has a shape that could itself become another worth-machine treadmill if execution/optimization becomes the new thing self-worth is proven through — the same pattern already named at `audit-framework` and `branch-self-worth-identity`. Worth watching, not assumed exempt just because the Map layer's stated purpose (freedom) sounds like the opposite of the worth-machine's usual currency (output).

## Form vs. Instrument

Form (poem, novel, biography, autobiography, scripture, myth) and epistemic instrument (philosophy / science / lived-experience / myth-form / religion / art) are different axes. A single work can carry more than one instrument at once. The epistemic_tag follows what content or function is actually being transmitted by the work for THIS reader — not which shelf the book sits on. Content and function can diverge: a factually true, documentary work (real people, real place) can still function as myth-form if its actual job for the reader is borrowing cultural meaning and connecting to land, rather than transmitting verifiable fact.

Worked examples: a biography of a scientist (Isaac Newton) tags science for its physics content, but lived-experience (mediated) for the life — secondhand, filtered through a biographer's construction. The Lord of the Rings tags myth-form alone — an explicitly built legendarium, not allegory. The Chronicles of Narnia tags myth-form and religion — Lewis deliberately encoded Christian theology into story. Pastoral-Australia works (Kings in Grass Castles, A Town Like Alice) tag lived-experience for their documented real lives and places, and myth-form for their actual function — borrowing cultural meaning and connecting to land.

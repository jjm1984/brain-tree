# Tree Structure Principles

These are the architectural rules the tree holds itself to. Not guidelines — operating constraints.

## Crossroads

When two ideas within the tree pull against each other without resolution — or when a philosophy could genuinely develop in more than one direction — this is marked as a CROSSROADS. Both paths are named. Neither is closed. The choosing is left visible.

A crossroads is not a flaw or a contradiction. It is a decision point where the direction taken reveals something about who you are. The reader is invited to draw their own conclusion.

## Counters

Every philosophy carries the traditions and positions that challenge or contradict it. A philosophy without its counters is not fully formed — it is a preference that has not been tested. Knowing what you push against is part of knowing what you believe.

Counters are held with equal weight to supporting ideas. They are named, not dismissed.

## Exposed Roots

Traditions that the philosophy sits near, resonates with, or was unknowingly shaped by. Marked honestly: some resonant, some peripheral, some unengaged. None claimed as sources unless explicitly adopted.

## Dear Reader

Where thinking is incomplete, original, or genuinely forked — a note is left for the reader. Space is held for them to dig deeper and form their own conclusions. The tree is a thinking partner, not an authority.

## Tension Holding

Where religion, science, philosophy, myth, and lived experience pull in genuinely different directions without resolution, this is named and left visible. Holding tension without forcing synthesis is a philosophical stance, not an evasion.

## Further Reading

Every branch knows its own edges. The further reading section names what lies beyond the tree's current reach. The tree is a map, not a destination. The further reading is the door. What is beyond it belongs to the reader.

## Everything goes in

Add everything — settled or not, coherent or not. Honest state labelling over false coherence. The Code layer must not be built prematurely — a partial code looks complete but has dangerous blind spots.

## The feedback loop is structural

The feedback loop is not supplementary to the tree. It is the mechanism by which the tree grows honestly. A tree that is never used against reality is a collection of preferences, not a philosophy. Gaps and contradictions appear not in the building phase but in the deployment phase.

## Peripheral leaves matter as much as green ones

The gap is named. The knowledge is not yet there. That is as valuable as a settled leaf — it shows where the roots have not yet reached.

## Form vs. Instrument

Form (poem, novel, biography, autobiography, scripture, myth) and epistemic instrument (philosophy / science / lived-experience / myth-form / religion / art) are different axes. A single work can carry more than one instrument at once. The epistemic_tag follows what content or function is actually being transmitted by the work for THIS reader — not which shelf the book sits on. Content and function can diverge: a factually true, documentary work (real people, real place) can still function as myth-form if its actual job for the reader is borrowing cultural meaning and connecting to land, rather than transmitting verifiable fact.

Worked examples: a biography of a scientist (Isaac Newton) tags science for its physics content, but lived-experience (mediated) for the life — secondhand, filtered through a biographer's construction. The Lord of the Rings tags myth-form alone — an explicitly built legendarium, not allegory. The Chronicles of Narnia tags myth-form and religion — Lewis deliberately encoded Christian theology into story. Pastoral-Australia works (Kings in Grass Castles, A Town Like Alice) tag lived-experience for their documented real lives and places, and myth-form for their actual function — borrowing cultural meaning and connecting to land.
